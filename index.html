Tips

Getting Around Japan

Suica / IC Card

Get a Suica (or Icoca) card at the airport on arrival — it works on virtually every train, subway, and bus in Japan. Also accepted at convenience stores and vending machines. You can add it to Apple Pay or Google Pay before landing.

Bike Rental — Kyoto

No reservation needed. Pick up bikes on April 17 (from 9:00) and return them on April 19 (by 17:00). Three full days of cycling through Kyoto's temples, paths, and backstreets.

Google Maps Works Great

Google Maps has excellent coverage of Japanese public transport including real-time schedules, platform numbers, and walking routes. Download the Kansai and Kanto offline maps before departure.

Ideas & Inspiration

Suggestions

Osaka

Minoo Quasi-National Park

45 min from Namba by train. A 2.7 km forest trail to a 33 m waterfall through cedar and maple forest. Try the momiji tempura (deep-fried maple leaves) at trail stalls. Best visited early morning.

Shinsekai Neighbourhood

Retro 1950s-styled neighbourhood. Famous for kushikatsu (fried skewers). Lively and authentic atmosphere. Great for an evening visit.

Kyoto

Fushimi Inari at Dawn

The full hike to Mt. Inari's summit (4 km, ~2 hrs) through thousands of vermillion torii gates. Open 24/7, no entry fee. The upper trails at dawn are nearly empty — an experience money can't buy. Best before sunrise.

Kurama to Kibune Mountain Trail

3.5 km mountain trail through ancient cedar forest. Visit Kibune Shrine with its water fortunes, then have kawadoko (river terrace) lunch over the river. End with Kurama Onsen — mountain hot springs surrounded by forest.

Kinkaku-ji & Ryoan-ji

The Gold Pavilion and Japan's most famous Zen rock garden are a 10-min walk apart. Go early (opens 9am) to beat crowds. The gold-leafed pavilion reflected in its mirror pond is iconic.

Fuji Area

Chureito Pagoda

398 steps to the iconic 5-storey pagoda with Mt. Fuji as backdrop. Cherry blossoms frame the view perfectly in mid-April. The single most photographed view in Japan.

Cycle Lake Kawaguchi

Rent bikes and circumnavigate the lake (20 km) with Fuji views throughout. The northern shore gives the classic reflection in still water. Combine with a visit to Aokigahara Forest.

Tokyo

TeamLab Planets

Immersive digital art — walk barefoot through infinite flower rooms, crystal universes, and wade through water with koi. Book tickets well in advance via teamlab.art.

Meiji Shrine & Shinjuku Gyoen

170 acres of urban forest hiding the shrine. Then visit Shinjuku Gyoen Garden (¥500) for stunning late-blooming yaezakura cherry trees, typically peaking around Apr 20–25.

Yanaka Old Town

Tokyo's most charming neighbourhood. Narrow lanes, vintage shopfronts, temple cemeteries, stray cats, almost no tourists. A genuine glimpse of old Tokyo.

Day Trip: Nikko

2 hrs from Asakusa by train. 97 m Kegon Waterfall, Lake Chuzenji at 1,269 m altitude, and the ornate UNESCO Tosho-gu Shrine in ancient cedar forest. Cherry blossoms still bloom at this altitude in late April.

Senso-ji & Asakusa

Tokyo's oldest temple, right near your hotel. Go before 8am for total serenity. The Nakamise shopping lane is great for gifts and snacks. Take a water bus along the Sumida River.

Shibuya Sky

360° rooftop observation deck — book ahead for sunset. Then watch the famous Shibuya Crossing chaos from above.

General Tips

Vegetarian Dining Tips

Look for restaurants with 精進料理 (shojin ryori / temple cuisine) or 菜食 signs. Kyoto is especially vegetarian-friendly. In Osaka, try the Namba area. Useful phrase: "Niku nashi de onegaishimasu" (without meat, please).

Cash & Payments

Japan is increasingly cashless but keep ¥10,000–¥20,000 in cash for smaller vendors, shrines, and rural areas. 7-Eleven ATMs accept international cards. Suica/IC cards handle most daily purchases.

Cherry Blossom Timing

You're in luck: Osaka & Kyoto will have late-blooming yaezakura around Apr 14–20. The Fuji Five Lakes area peaks mid-April — your timing is perfect. Tokyo may still have scattered late bloomers in Shinjuku Gyoen.
